History of Boba

The birth of Boba explained.

Boba: Origin Story

Boba (n) - a cold, frothy drink made with iced tea, sweetened milk or other flavorings, and usually with sweet black balls or “pearls” made from tapioca. -Google definitions

Trending Craze

Boba tea, a popular drink originating from Taiwan, has been a trending craze among both young and old for almost three decades. Created in the 80s, true credit for the fashionable drink's origins are unknown.

  • Learn more

The Origins

Tea shop owner Liu Han-Chieh and his product development manager, Lin Hsui Hui, are one group accredited to introducing boba when they decided to put tapioca pearls in their drinks. Their customers loved it and others started creating shops to sell the new drink.

  • Learn more

New Creations

Boba tea has evolved to more than just cold tea with milk and tapioca pearls. Now, there are are hot drinks, frozen drinks, yogurt drinks, slushie drinks, regular sugar, less sugar, boba, jelly, agar, pudding, and much, much more.

  • Learn more

Now

Eventually, it has became normal to see people walking along the street, chatting with friends, and drinking a cup of boba. Young kids and teenagers go to boba shops after school as a place where they can hang out, chill, and grab a bite to eat. Its colorful liquid and chewy bubbles have managed to attract the attention of many.
